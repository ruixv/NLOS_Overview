## Why it belongs in the survey

This is a direct extension of the FFT-accelerated phasor-field branch rather than a paper that cites NLOS only in passing. It analyzes spatial oversampling in common relay-wall measurements and introduces:

- NUFFT reconstruction from sparse, irregular relay samples and arbitrarily shaped relay regions;
- NUFFT evaluation at arbitrary hidden-volume locations, decoupling input and output grids;
- scaled FFT reconstruction of larger hidden volumes without increasing stored samples;
- FFT-like computational complexity throughout.

It therefore connects core phasor-field and Fourier-domain reconstruction to practical SPAD-array acquisition, irregular sampling, flexible output grids, and memory-aware deployment.
