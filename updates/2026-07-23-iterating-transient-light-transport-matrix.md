## Relevance and contribution

This is a direct active time-of-flight NLOS imaging paper. Conventional systems measure lower-dimensional subsets of the transient light-transport matrix and primarily recover hidden geometry. Sultan et al. densely scan relay-wall illumination while a gated 16×16 SPAD array records multiple relay-wall detection positions, sampling the full first-order relay-surface TLTM (TLTM-1). Phasor-field beamforming then focuses virtual illumination and detection independently within the hidden scene to recover TLTM-2, the time-resolved transport between hidden-surface patches.

The recovered higher-order transport reveals indirect shadows, interreflections, and volumetric scattering and enables direct/indirect separation, virtual relighting, and dual photography. It is therefore a milestone after phasor fields, virtual light-transport matrices, and higher-order virtual-mirror transport: active NLOS advances from estimating only hidden geometry toward computationally measuring transport inside the hidden scene itself.
