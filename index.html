Development Timeline

A compact reading path for understanding the field's historical trajectory.

2008
5D time-light transport

The conceptual foundation for using transient light transport to sense hidden scenes.

2012
First experimental 3D NLOS reconstruction

Velten et al. used ultrafast ToF measurements and filtered backprojection to reconstruct hidden 3D shape.

2018
Confocal LCT and virtual-wave thinking

Light-cone transform and phasor-field formulations made NLOS reconstruction faster and more wave-optics aware.

2019
f-k migration and passive periscopy

The field split into efficient wave-based active NLOS and ordinary-camera passive NLOS.

2021
Kilometer range, neural fields, picosecond timing, and keyhole imaging

Long-range NLOS, NeTF, PRL picosecond-resolution up-conversion detection, and single-optical-path keyhole imaging expanded both scale and acquisition regimes.

2022
Sub-mm resolution, event cameras, THz NLOS, and few-shot acquisition

UNCOVER pushed active resolution; event cameras opened moving-target passive NLOS; THz systems explored lossy-mirror around-obstacle imaging; SSCR showed sparse few-shot active recovery.

2023
Transformers, arbitrary patterns, and active corner cameras

NLOST, PAC-Net, NLOS-NeuS, arbitrary illumination/detection reconstruction, structure-sparsity regularization, and active corner-camera background mapping broadened reconstruction settings.

2024
Real-time video, TLTM iteration, ptychography, autonomous navigation, and EM skins

Dynamic room-sized NLOS video, Mamba-style temporal modeling, TLTM iteration, ptychographic depth-resolved NLOS, single-photon-LiDAR navigation, unsupervised virtual scanning, SSD, full-color passive 3D NLOS, and passive metasurface-assisted radar NLOS became central.

2025
Operator learning, pretraining, radar, acoustic, and robotic NLOS

TransiT, graph models, HoloRadar, NANO, MARMOT, TRT, Quasi-Fresnel transforms, MITO, N2LoS, mmMirror, See and Beam, SuperEx, mitransient, and relay-free acoustic NLOS broadened the toolbox.

2026
Arbitrary relay surfaces, consumer LiDAR, RIS, RF geometry, and distributed MIMO imaging

The frontier moves toward real-world geometry: non-planar limited relays, smartphone-grade LiDAR motion sampling, RF/radar neural reconstruction, low-cost LiDAR datasets, RIS/EMVS localization, backscatter positioning, double-bounce radio SLAM, and NLOS-aided off-grid MIMO/ISAC imaging.

How to Read the Field

The repository is organized as a roadmap rather than a flat bibliography.

1. Physical model

  • ToF ellipsoids and light-cone transform
  • Wave / phasor / f-k migration
  • Differentiable transient rendering and TLTM iteration

2. Acquisition regime

  • Dense scanning versus sparse sampling
  • Scannerless / real-time / active-corner systems
  • Motion-induced, irregular, few-shot, and single-path sampling

3. Sensor modality

  • SPAD, ToF, LiDAR, camera
  • Radar / RF / mmWave / THz / ISAC
  • Acoustic, ultrasound, event camera

4. Reconstruction target

  • Albedo, volume, surface, mesh
  • Motion, tracking, human pose
  • Mapping, navigation, localization

5. Learning paradigm

  • CNN / transformer / Mamba / GNN
  • Physics-guided priors and neural operators
  • Neural implicit, Gaussian, and simulation tools

6. Real-world frontier

  • Non-planar and limited relay surfaces
  • Arbitrary illumination / detection patterns
  • Low-cost mobile, RF, THz, metasurface, and robotic hardware
